Welcome

Welcome to the Sensors & Functional Materials website. The website is primarily intended for internal use. A place where we store relevant information and details for current and future group members. However, we believe that some of it may be of general interest. Therefore, everything is published openly.

We are a small sub-group working at DTU Offshore, The Technical University of Denmark. Our primary focus is the development and application of porous materials to solve real-life problems related to the offshore and energy transition industry (e.g., sensing, separations, activation). We are also passionate about using mass spectrometry to probe complex systems and understand chemical processes. Our research is mission driven, meaning that it lies on the interface between fundamental and applied science. You can read more about this and ongoing project on the ‘Research Vision’ page.

Collaborations?

If you found your way here, and believe that what we’re working on sounds interesting, please don’t hesitate in sending us an e-mail. We’re always happy to collaborate. We’re also open for B.Sc. and M.Sc. thesis projects. If you’re a student at DTU we are also happy to develop and teach special courses (credit awarding) tailored to your interest.

Potential thesis topics

  • Sensor development for in-situ environmental monitoring (e.g., persistent organic pollutants, marine nutrients)
  • Oxygen chemisorption for industrial processes (e.g., oxyfuel combustion, deoxygenation of process fluids)
  • Advanced chromatography and mass spectrometry (e.g., non-target screening and quantification of pollutants, process analytics)

Please note: Any Ph.D. or PD positions will be posted on the DTU website. We can not accept applications via e-mail. If you are interest in co-writing a grant application, we are open for discussion.
